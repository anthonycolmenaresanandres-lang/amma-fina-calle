## 1. Prestige DNA Summary

Colattao should read as a premium local cafe brand with cinematic restraint, not a generic food promo.

Prestige constants for this packet:

- Mobile-first 9:16 quality is the primary format.
- 4:5 feed crops are secondary but should still feel editorial.
- Visual hierarchy should be simple: environment, product, owner reference, then overlay.
- Espresso, amber, cream, dark wood, brick, muted gold, and blue-white ceramic details should carry the look.
- Use fewer stronger elements instead of busy collage layouts.
- Digital Menu language must stay controlled and never be replaced with website terminology.
- Logos, QR codes, CTA text, menu words, Digital Menu copy, and prices remain overlays only.
- No AI-generated logos, QR codes, readable menu copy, CTA text, legal text, or pricing.

The owner/spokesperson should function as a premium hospitality presence, not as a final generated identity yet.

Allowed role in first mockups:

- Internal storyboard reference.
- Composition planning reference.
- Wardrobe/presence reference.

## 4. Environment Mood

Primary environment mood should be warm, curated, and intimate.

Use:

- Brick, chandelier, plant, shelf decor, ceramic blue-white accents, dark wood, warm amber light.
- Slow cafe texture rather than fast promotional clutter.
- Fireplace/fogata references as atmosphere accents only until reviewed.
- Shelf decor and plant details as safe premium continuity shots.

Avoid:

- Generic cafe stock imagery.
- Cold white lighting.
- Overcrowded coffee-shop scenes.
- Baked readable slogans or text inside generated or processed visuals.
- Using B001-A13 as the opening hero before human review.

## 5. Product Hero Mood

Product mood should feel indulgent, warm, and editorial.

Best first product direction:

- Coffee + pastry as the lead product pairing.
- Blue-white cup and plate details as brand texture.
- Warm wood and amber highlights as the visual bridge to the environment.
- Product should be large enough to read on mobile but not cropped into a chaotic close-up.

Use B001-A01 as the first product hero candidate because it already has strong lighting, clear subject hierarchy, and premium cafe styling.

## 6. Color / Lighting Direction

Core palette:

- Espresso brown.
- Dark wood.
- Warm amber.
- Cream/parchment.
- Muted antique gold.
- Brick red-brown.
- Blue-white ceramic accent.
- Deep shadow/black for contrast.

Lighting rules:

- Warm directional light is preferred.
- Highlights should feel candle/chandelier/fireplace-adjacent, not neon.
- Shadows should preserve depth without hiding product or owner face.
- Avoid over-bright gold, saturated yellow, artificial HDR, or plastic-smooth render light.

## 7. 9:16 Vertical Composition Rules

Primary vertical structure:

1. Top safe zone: optional logo/CTA overlay area only, not baked into image.
2. Middle hero zone: product, environment detail, or owner reference subject.
3. Lower safe zone: QR, Digital Menu CTA, Cafe Rush CTA, or footer signature overlay.

Rules:

- Leave breathing room for overlay placement.
- Do not place owner face or product detail under future QR/CTA zones.
- Keep the strongest product or detail in the center third.
- Use shelf/plant/fogata details as transitional vertical inserts.
- Avoid tiny product subjects on mobile.
- Avoid more than one heavy visual idea per frame.
- If using B001-A13, frame it as a detail/mood insert only and mark review required.

Recommended 9:16 sequence skeleton:

1. Environment detail hook: plant or shelf decor.
2. Product reveal: coffee + pastry vertical teaser.
3. Digital Menu proof frame: approved Digital Menu screenshot or approved menu board crop only.
4. Interaction CTA: overlay-only QR or Digital Menu/Cafe Rush CTA.
5. Signature/end-card: logo/footer/signature overlays only.

## 8. 4:5 Feed Composition Rules

4:5 feed posts should feel like premium editorial stills.

Rules:

- Product hero can fill more of the frame than 9:16.
- Keep composition calmer and less informational than story frames.
- Use 4:5 product hero as the first public-facing still candidate.
- Use plant/shelf decor as supporting carousel frames.
- Do not cram QR, CTA, logo, and product into one frame unless it is a final poster layout.
- Keep all readable text as overlay-controlled elements.

Recommended 4:5 carousel structure:

1. Coffee + pastry hero.
2. Shelf decor detail.
3. Plant atmosphere detail.
4. Digital Menu proof crop.
5. CTA/QR overlay frame.

## 9. Overlay Placement Rules

Overlay-only elements:

- Colattao logo.
- Fina Calle mark if used.
- QR codes.
- CTA text.
- Digital Menu words and menu copy.
- Cafe Rush CTA.
- Footer/signature marks.
- Legal/safety copy.
- Pricing.

Overlay safe zones:

- 9:16 top: logo or short CTA only, with generous margins.
- 9:16 bottom: QR and CTA placement, never covering the product hero or face.
- 4:5 top/bottom: simple logo or CTA; avoid blocking food or environment texture.
- Use high contrast but restrained overlay treatment: parchment, espresso, muted gold, cream, or black translucent panels.

Forbidden overlay behavior:

- Do not generate readable text inside image/video generation.
- Do not use AI-generated QR codes.
- Do not extract a blurred in-scene logo as a final clean overlay.
- Do not place QR over busy product texture unless scan-tested.
- Do not call Colattao Digital Menu a website in campaign copy.

## 10. Rejected Visual Choices

Reject directions that feel like:

- Generic agency cafe ad.
- Coupon, discount, or loud promo flyer.
- Neon nightlife aesthetic.
- Overly glossy AI food render without hospitality context.
- Fake luxury with excessive gold effects.
- Busy collage of owner, food, QR, logo, and menu at once.
- Random cafe background unrelated to Colattao mood.
- Generated readable logo, QR, CTA, menu words, or Digital Menu copy.
- Baked visible text in environment/product images unless it is an approved screenshot or overlay.
- Final owner/spokesperson generation before identity lock approval.
