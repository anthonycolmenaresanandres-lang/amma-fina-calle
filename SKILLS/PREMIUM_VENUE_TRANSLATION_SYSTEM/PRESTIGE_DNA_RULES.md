# Prestige DNA Rules

## Purpose

Prestige DNA is the fixed visual and production standard that carries across Fina Calle OS premium venue campaigns.

These rules are constants. They should remain stable even when the restaurant identity, food, colors, owner, and environment change.

## Prestige Constants

### 1. Premium Visual Tone

- The campaign must feel high-end, intentional, and curated.
- Avoid generic startup polish, cluttered promos, cheap gradients, and loud coupon aesthetics.
- Visuals should feel like a premium hospitality brand, not a random AI render.

### 2. Elegant Cinematic Composition

- Use controlled framing.
- Prioritize strong subject hierarchy.
- Keep camera language restrained.
- Use depth, light, and contrast intentionally.
- Avoid chaotic movement or over-decorated scenes.

### 3. Clean Overlay Discipline

- Logos are overlays only.
- QR codes are overlays only.
- CTA text is overlay only.
- Digital Menu text and menu copy are overlays or approved screenshots only.
- No generated image/video output should contain final readable brand text.

### 4. Mobile-First 9:16 Quality

- Every campaign must work on a phone first.
- Safe areas must protect faces, products, QR codes, and CTA overlays.
- Visual detail must remain readable at mobile size.
- 4:5 and 1:1 crops are secondary exports, not the primary creative standard.

### 5. Restrained Luxury

- Use fewer, stronger elements.
- Let product, owner, and environment breathe.
- Prefer quiet confidence over exaggerated effects.
- Gold, black, espresso, parchment, cream, bronze, and deep accent colors must be used with restraint.

### 6. Consistency Over Novelty

- Repetition builds brand memory.
- Reuse approved overlay placements.
- Reuse approved product hero positions.
- Reuse approved end-card structures.
- Avoid changing visual language just to make something feel new.

### 7. Asset Approval Before Generation

- Owner identity must be approved before any owner/spokesperson generation.
- Venue environment must be approved before environment-specific generation.
- Product hero assets must be approved before product-focused generation.
- QR destinations must be verified before QR overlays.

### 8. No AI-Generated Identity Artifacts

The following are rejected if AI-generated as final readable elements:

- Logos.
- QR codes.
- Digital Menu copy.
- Menu copy.
- CTA text.
- Legal disclaimers.
- Pricing.
- Owner identity without an approved lock.

## Prestige Failure Modes

Reject or revise if the campaign feels:

- Generic.
- Overproduced.
- Too busy.
- Cheap or coupon-like.
- Inconsistent with the venue.
- Dependent on hallucinated brand details.
- Hard to read on mobile.
- Like Colattao was cloned instead of translated.
