# Scylla Sequence Case Study

## Purpose

Demonstrate how Fina Calle OS cinematic rules handle a complex monster sequence with vertical framing, environmental masking, identity locks, and one-motion shot discipline.

## SHOT 01: The Trap

Z-Axis Placement: Extreme Wide Z-Axis. Infinite depth.
Subject Action: Massive violently spinning black whirlpool, white foam crashing, no humans visible.
Camera Movement: Slow drone-style tilt down.
Atmosphere: Underexposed, heavy rain, strobe lightning, thick fog.
Final Prompt:
[SUBJECT: massive violently spinning black ocean whirlpool, crashing white foam, churning water physics] + [CAMERA: slow tilt down, high angle] + [ATMOSPHERE: underexposed, heavy rain, strobe lightning illumination, thick fog] + [FORMAT: 9:16 aspect ratio, shot on 16mm film, Kodak Vision3 500T, heavy film grain, cinematic realism]

Atmosphere: Harsh directional strobe lightning from top right, deep shadows left side of face, volumetric rain.
